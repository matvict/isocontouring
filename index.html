This demo application allows to explore the effect of isocontouring transfer function on a several example images. For the details on the technique please refer to the publication: Victor Matvienko and Jens Krueger, Dense Isocontour Imaging, Proceedings ACM SIGGRAPH Asia 2013 (Technical Briefs).

The original image is combined with a transfer function, applied to a its Gaussian-smoothed intensity image in two ways: blending with parameter alpha, specifying the relative weight of each image, and multiplication of the two images.

The magic lens allows to view the effect of combination of images locally within the lense radius with the original image in the background or vice versa in the flipped mode. For detailed exploration zooming and panning operations are available.

The presented images are released in public domain and can be found in the Wikimedia Commons database.

For correct work, the demo requires a WebGL-compatible browser, Google Chrome is recommended. For any questions, please contact Victor Matvienko: matvict@gmail.com
